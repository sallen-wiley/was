Widgets

Content cards

Presenting various types of content in a range of configured feeds

Throughout the Wiley Analytical Science site, variouse types of content ranging from articles to webinars, presents itself in content 'feeds'; such as latest news, upcoming events, or featured articles. The way this content is displayed is with a flexible widget that identifies the content type and then presents the relative information in a 'card'. The cards are fluid elements that can stretch to fill the width of the container and expand downwards to fit the required information.

Cards are used in the following locations:

  • Content bands on homepages (WAS, subjects)
  • Search results
  • Related content widgets

Card type

Horizontal

Better suited to layouts where content order is important. Such as search results and events listings. Note that the extra, reliable width means that we can bring the author name up to the top row to reduce unecessary card height.

Also note the smaller feature image which will be restricted to a width of ~33% the card and cropped depending on the height determined by the title and snippet. The feature image is dropped on XS breakpoints.

Short cards

To ensure lists of content use space efficiently, available image space is subject to the copy provided. 

Depending on the length of the title and the presence of short abstracts or snippets, the height of the card can change. Providing we know if the image can be cropped or needs to be entirley visible, we should aim to provide an optimised experience regardless of the content present.

Vertical

Better suited to lean-back (browsing) experiences. Such as most read, featured, or latest content. Note the capacity for larger, uncropped images. Due to the smaller width compared to the horizontal card, the author/publication is dropped below the title and snippet or above the feature image to avoid awkward wrapping. 

Landscape images

Landscape images are permitted to fill the horizontal space. In the event the card is wider than 300px landscape images will be cropped or centrally aligned depending on whether it is set to be cropped or contained.

Card Anatomy

Type

The content type is indicated in the top left of the card. A card can only have one content type. For example 'Article' or 'News'.

The content type should not display if all cards within the content feed are configured to be the same content type. Example, The WAS home has a section titled 'Latest Articles', all content within this widget should be of the article type and therfore it would be redundant to repeat the content type within each card.

Subjects

We display the associated subjects to the right of the content type. We are proposing that Authors and Editors never assign more than 3 subjects to a piece of content.

  • Individual subjects composed of multiple words cannot wrap. Otherwise adjacent subjects would run into eachother.
  • If the container is not wide enough, subjects can wrap below eachother.
  • Subjects should not wrap below the content type.
  • We display all related subjects on the following page types and in the following widgets:
    • WAS home
    • Search results
    • Related content widgets
  • We display all T2 subjects related to the parent T1 subject on T1 subject homepages. Eg. Microscopy home
  • We do not display subjects on T2 homepages. Eg. Light Microscopy home

Title

Titles act as links to the content view and should use a call-to-action colour. The follow rules apply to determine the title color:

  • Titles within cards on the WAS homepage are rendered in Burgandy
  • Titles within cards on the T1 and T2 subject homepages should be rendered in that subject's color. 
    • Microscopy
    • Spectroscopy
    • Separation Science
    • Equipment
    • Lab Automation
  • Title within cards on search results pages are rendered in Burgandy
  • Titles within cards in related content widgets are rendered in Burgandy

Snippet

Snippets are short passages of text that give a little more information about the content.

  • Snippets are shown on:
    • WAS and subject homepages
    • Search results
  • Snippets are not shown on:
    • Related content widgets

Author or Publication

Content can have authors and/or publications associated to them.

A content card can show either a publication or an author. Not both.

If a piece of content has an associated publication and authors, we only show the title of the publication.

If there is no associated publication, we show the first author. If there are multiple authors, we show the name of the first author and append 'et al.'.

If there is no publication or author, we show nothing in this space.

Publication Date

The date should render both the day and month the content was published.

If the content was published in a calendar year different to the current year, we also show that year.
