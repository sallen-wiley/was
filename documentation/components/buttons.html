General Rules

Color should be used sparingly and consistently throughout Wiley Analytical Science. The sensitive nature of the material and the importance of identity to the primary topic areas requires the color palette on WAS be supportive of the content and not work against it. Some examples of how certain colors should be used are outlined below. 

Normal buttons

In most situations, the primary color should be used for call-to-actions. The secondary color can be used to help present a hierarchy of preference or distinguish more common actions from less common actions. The standard 'link' style CTA can also be used.

Other colors should only be considered when trying to reinforce certain styles of messaging; ie. Success or alerts. Or if buttons are being used on top of images.
