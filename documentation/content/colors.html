Colors

Documentation and uses for the Wiley Analytical Science Library color palette

General Rules

Color should be used sparingly and consistently throughout Wiley Analytical Science. The sensitive nature of the material and the importance of identity to our partners requires the color palette on W.A.S. be supportive of their content and not work against it. Some examples of how certain colors should be used are outlined below. 

Due to vast range of content and functionality, it is perfectly acceptable to use any of the colors below for text, backgrounds, borders, etc. to accommodate the needs of the content and the user. However, please take note of the following:

  • Avoid using the primary color on text that is not a link - this can mislead the user in thinking that it is a link. 
  • Avoid using success green or danger red in close proximity to forms or other types of alert messaging - this color is used in form validation and communicating messages to the user.
